What to Expect

An electrical apprenticeship aptitude test consists of two main sections:

  • Math Section: 45 minutes of algebra, functions, number series, and mathematical reasoning. The math sections on this site are longer than the actual test, so they use a 50 minute time limit.
  • Reading Comprehension: 50 minutes of understanding written passages and answering questions
  • Additional Topics: Depending on the test, you may also be tested on other topics such as:
    • Mechanical Aptitude
    • Spatial Reasoning (paper folding)
    • Other topics may vary by test

These practice exams are designed to help you familiarize yourself with the math and readingquestion types and format you'll encounter on test day. Additional topics may be tested on the test day, so be sure to practice those as well.

How to Use This Site

  1. Start with a full practice exam: Take Exam 1 and mimic the test environment. You will only be allowed to have scratch paper and a pencil. Get those then set a timer for 50 minutes for the math portion. Note that there are no calculators allowed. Take a 10-minute break, then spend 50 minutes on the reading portion.
  2. Review: After completing the exam, grade your answers and review the explanations for each problem.
  3. Study: Go check out the Math Help page and make sure you understand the strategies for solving each type of question. Look over the Reading Tips page as well.
  4. Practice sections separately: Use the Math Practice and Reading Practice pages to focus on specific areas where you need improvement in math, and get additional practice in reading.
  5. Get extra practice and explanations: If you still need to practice more, I recommend that you use an LLM like Chat GPT or Claude to get extra practice questions and explanations.
  6. Take the second exam: 1-2 days before your test, complete Exam 2 to measure your progress and further prepare for test day.
